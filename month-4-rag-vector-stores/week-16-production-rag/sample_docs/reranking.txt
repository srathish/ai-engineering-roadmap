Reranking in a RAG Pipeline

Retrieval by embedding similarity is fast but blunt. The closest vectors are
usually relevant, but not always in the best order, and a chunk can be
semantically near a query while not actually containing the answer. Reranking
is a second pass that re-sorts the retrieved candidates before they reach the
model.

The simplest reranker blends signals. You keep the dense embedding score and
add a lexical signal such as keyword overlap: how many of the query's words
actually appear in the chunk. A weighted combination of the two often beats
either alone, because it rewards chunks that are both semantically close and
literally on-topic.

A threshold step is just as important. After reranking you drop any candidate
whose score falls below a floor, so weak matches never reach the prompt. This
keeps token usage down and stops irrelevant context from confusing the model.
More advanced systems use a dedicated cross-encoder reranker model, but the
idea is the same: retrieve broadly, then re-sort and prune before generating.
