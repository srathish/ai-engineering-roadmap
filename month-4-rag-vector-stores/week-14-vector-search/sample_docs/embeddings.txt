Embeddings and Vector Search

An embedding is a list of numbers - a vector - that represents a piece of
text in a way that captures its meaning. Texts with similar meaning land near
each other in this vector space, even if they share no words. "How do I reset
my password" and "I forgot my login credentials" point in nearly the same
direction.

To compare two embeddings you measure the angle between them, not the distance.
Cosine similarity is the standard measure: it is the dot product of the two
vectors after they have been normalized to unit length. A score of 1 means the
vectors point the same way, 0 means they are unrelated, and negative values
mean they point in opposite directions.

Vector search works by embedding every chunk in your corpus once, up front,
and storing the vectors. At query time you embed the question and find the
chunks whose vectors are closest to it. With a small corpus you can compare
the query against every stored vector - a brute-force scan. That is exactly
what an in-memory numpy implementation does.
